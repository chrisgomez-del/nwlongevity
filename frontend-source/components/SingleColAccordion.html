FAQs

What is the Northwestern Medicine West Health Accelerator?

Integrated behavioral health (IBH) refers to mental healthcare models that use primary care practices to deliver treatment. Unlike traditional healthcare delivery tactics, which rely on specialist referrals, patients can get treatment for mental health concerns directly through their primary care provider. This involves an initial mental health screening at a primary care visit, and where patients identified as needing mental health services can receive them through programs in their primary care setting.

This method of treatment avoids lost referral follow-ups, providing greater assurance that patients get the care they need, when they need it.
